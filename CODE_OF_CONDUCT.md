# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This project welcomes contributions from anyone, regardless of experience
level, background, or identity. We expect everyone participating —
maintainers, contributors, and issue/PR commenters alike — to treat each
other with respect.

**Expected behavior:**
- Be respectful and constructive in feedback, especially in code review.
- Assume good faith — most disagreements are about the best technical
  approach, not personal.
- Focus criticism on the code and the idea, not the person who wrote it.
- Accept that maintainers may decline a contribution or request changes;
  disagreement is fine, hostility is not.

**Unacceptable behavior:**
- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks, in any project space (issues,
  PRs, discussions, commit messages).
- Discriminatory jokes or language.
- Publishing others' private information without consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions or reviews.

## Scope

This applies within all project spaces (this repository's issues, pull
requests, and discussions) and when representing the project elsewhere.

## Enforcement

Violations may be reported by opening an issue tagged for maintainer
attention, or — for reports involving sensitive details you'd rather not
post publicly — via GitHub's private vulnerability reporting channel
described in [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) as a fallback contact path.

Maintainers will review reports and may take any action they judge
appropriate, including warning a contributor, removing comments, or
blocking further participation for repeat or severe violations.
